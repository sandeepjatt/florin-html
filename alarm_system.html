How much is an alarm system?

The price structure for alarm systems is immense at first glance. In hardware stores and Internet shops today "alarm sets" can be purchased for amounts of well below 500 €. In contrast, installations installed by specialized engineers or specialized companies often cost five-digit amounts. Why is that?
Alarm systems, which are to protect against burglary and theft, are technically correctly called burglary alarm systems. The alarm systems also include fire alarm systems.
Intruder alarm systems are in accordance with DIN VDE 0833 in four classes, which are referred to as grades. The higher the level of an alarm system, the harder it is to overcome.

Grad1 - are below the police requirements

Grade 1 is the lowest grade and is kept so simple that it can be redefined as the sump for non-Grade 2 facilities. Grade 1 facilities are inexpensive and often easy to install. They enjoy increasing popularity among private consumers due to technical developments such as wireless technology and smart home systems. An exemplary representative of the segment is the ABUS Privest, which can be obtained as a basic set for about 300 euros on the Internet or in hardware stores. However, prospective buyers should take two things into account: the basic sets are very limited in their features. For example, the ABUS set contains only one opening detector. For each additional door or each additional window) further detectors are necessary, which are to be set per piece with 30 - 50 euros. Thus, even with self-assembly and a low number of detectors, most Grade 1 alarm systems can not be realized at under € 500. More seriously, the first-degree facilities offer very little protection. The German police writes on its website: "Please note: Grade 1 burglar alarm systems are below the police requirements - they are not recommended by the police."

Grade 2 for normal houses and apartments

Grade 2 Intrusion Alarm Systems are the minimum recommended by the police for "normal" homes and dwellings. VdS Schadenverhütung GmbH, which is a company of the German Insurance Association, translates this standard largely into the VdS class A, which manufacturers can acquire in certification by the VdS. An alarm system of VdS class A is also required from a certain insurance volume (often 200,000 euros) in many household contents insurance. There are significant price differences between Grade 1 and Grade 2 burglar alarm systems. For example, ABUS Secvest, which complies with degree 2, costs more than four times as much in the basic set of 1300 euros as the Privest model described above. In degree 2, the use of motion detectors is already provided in addition to the use of opening sensors.

Grade 3 for commercial area

Grade 3 burglar alarm systems are less frequently used in the private sector, but are recommended for special hazardous situations (eg larger values). In the commercial sector, however, these alarm systems are very common. Grade 3 is often used synonymously with VdS class B. However, it should be borne in mind that this comparison holds only in very broad terms. The VdS classification is considered more finely divided and extensive, which is why it is also used much more widely in Germany. While radio technology is still widely used in VdS class A, with the Daitem 24000 there is currently only one wireless alarm system that has certification to VdS 24000. Often, the installation of such alarm systems already requires several days. Alarm systems of the VdS class B can, depending on the configuration, quickly exceed the budget of 10,000 euros.

Grade 4 for commercial risks (jewelers, banks, galleries)

Grade 4 burglar alarm systems are the highest class. They are mainly used for special commercial risks (jewelers, banks, galleries, ...) and public institutions. Grade 4 is roughly equated with VdS class C. However, these are divided into four groups of damage (3 to 6), which makes it clear that a mere equate takes too short.
