Video surveillance: What technologies are there?

With the global growth in video surveillance technology and the rapid advances in digital technology, video surveillance systems are becoming increasingly affordable and are becoming increasingly popular in the home and small business sectors.
Video surveillance systems are not only used in security technology, but are also used in the pursuit of operational processes (for example in warehousing or manufacturing), in scientific research or in the context of the control of infants, carers and pets.
The spectrum of technology used is very broad. In addition to the question of where and how many cameras to install for a system, there are numerous other technical issues to be resolved.

Analog or digital?

In video surveillance, too, the trend is towards digital technology and thus high-resolution IP cameras. Digital technology is suitable for higher storage volumes and for the use of standard PC hardware. Analog cameras therefore play an increasingly subordinate role.

What types of surveillance cameras should be used?

Boxed-Type surveillance cameras and bullet-style cameras record events in a room they are fixed on. The viewing direction of these cameras is clearly visible from the outside. Boxed-type cameras have a rectangular shape, while bullet-style cameras have a tubular housing.
Fixed Dome cameras have the advantage that you are not immediately looking at the line of sight, because the lens is behind a dome-like housing.
Both the first mentioned camera types as well as the fixed dome cameras are available as swiveling systems. In the case of dome cameras we speak of PTZ dome cameras (PTZ = pan, tilt & zoom = pan, tilt and zoom).
If permanent monitoring of a 360-degree range is required, you can use panorama cameras for this purpose.

Which lighting conditions must video surveillance be adapted to?

If surveillance cameras are to work in the dark, then you can use day / night cameras that record near-infrared light to create black-and-white images when the brightness drops below a certain threshold.
An alternative is to work with lighting technology that illuminates a dark area that can be used with cameras without particularly high photosensitivity.
Finally, there are still infrared cameras that can be used even in total darkness, because they record heat radiation.
Often it is overlooked that even very bright sceneries (backlighting) for surveillance cameras can be a big problem. Cameras that use Wide Dynamic Range (WDR) technology will shoot much better than cameras that do not have such technology. Advanced WDR features like WDR - Dynamic Capture and WDR - Forensic Capture are designed for the most demanding scenarios.

Video surveillance: almost always wired

WLAN offers sufficient bandwidth for the secure transmission of images only for low-resolution entry-level cameras. Accordingly, professional surveillance systems are still wired today. In the high-end range, however, you can make the camera settings via WLAN-enabled installation devices on selected models.
