Smoke detector: Pratical little heroes

TOn an average day there are about 550 fires in Germany. They cause considerable property damage, but above all personal injury. 2/3 of the dead and injured are surprised by a fire at night. 80 - 90% of the dead fall victim to the flue gases and not to the flames. Timely warning by smoke detectors could prevent many personal injury.

Detectors are small fire alarm systems that consist of two components, namely a detecting and an alarming unit. They are reasonably priced and in many cases can simply be mounted under the ceiling using adhesive tape. The operating principle is trivial: if gases penetrate into the measuring chamber of the smoke detector, it emits an alarm sound of at least 85 dB. In the past ten years, more and more federal states have begun to make smoke detectors in new and, at different times, mandatory in existing buildings. As the last federal state, Berlin moved to the beginning of 2017 and introduced a duty for new buildings. For existing buildings, a retrofit obligation is planned until the end of 2020.

Where are the smoke detectors required?

Since the scheme is in the sovereignty of the countries, there are regional differences in the regulations. However, the new Berlin building regulations can be taken as a good point of reference for what is recommended and in many states so demanded. It provides in §48 that living rooms, except kitchens and corridors, must pass through the escape routes and must be equipped with at least one smoke detector.

What to look for when purchasing smoke detectors?

Alarm system-pro-professional recommends when purchasing generally to make sure that the detectors are equipped with wireless and a 10-year battery. Smoke detectors must bear the CE mark, including a test number and the specification EN 14604. Only then do they meet the minimum requirements of a smoke alarm device. A meaningful additional orientation is provided by the quality mark "Q", which identifies smoke detectors that fulfill certain minimum criteria with regard to their longevity and susceptibility to false alarms.
