Mist systems: With protective mist against burglary

With installations in department stores and retail outlets showing that the explosion of protective fog is causing many burglars to stop their act, systems are now also on the rise in the private sector. Alarmanlage--om-Profi.de has collected an overview of important facts on the subject of protective fog systems.

How do fog systems work?

When triggered, protective mist systems vaporize up to 50 cubic meters of fog per second and emit an extremely dense fog mixture. As you can see in the video from the Italian manufacturer UR FOG, it only takes a few seconds for the smallest model from the manufacturer (UR FOG Easy-1) to fog up a room.

Can the protective mist leave residues?

The smoke fluid is tested to European standards, absolutely safe and leaves no residue. In the basic composition, the same fog fluids are used, which are known from the fog machines used in show business, with protective fog machines only fluid mixtures are used, which have very high density and very long service life.

How much do protective fog systems cost?

With the UR FOG Easy-1, there is a new entry-level system on the market, which can be purchased at Alarmanlage-vom-Profi.de already 650 euros (including VAT). Larger systems from UR FOG or white safe cost four digits.

Do protective mist systems need a power connection?

Protective fog systems require a mains connection. They consume electricity permanently because the protective mist has to be heated. However, the systems are equipped with an emergency power supply, so that a power failure can not prevent the systems from triggering or fogging.

How do you install protective fog systems?

Shielding systems can be installed as pedestal systems or attached to walls and ceilings. Depending on their size, the systems weigh between 10 and 30 kg and allow fogging of 300 - 1800 cubic meters. Protection systems require a mains connection and are filled with fog cartridges. Their tripping can be brought about by a remote control or by signal of an alarm system. The systems are self-assembling.
